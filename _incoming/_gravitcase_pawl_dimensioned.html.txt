 The self-locking trick (View A). The fold-back reaction runs straight along the contact normal into the pivot pin — so the load is carried in pin shear , not as a moment trying to swing the pawl open, and a backstop catches any residual. The load can't cam it open; only the wedge can. The release (View B) is a separate, light path: the rod pushes the wedge under the heel, the 33° ramp lifts the nose 4 mm to clear the lug — and since you only stow when there's no fold-back load, the wedge fights only the return spring (~40 N), not the 5,500 N hold. Heavy path and release path are fully separated.
