 Joint Core — Master Definition 
 Gravitcase · COM-height pivot + 45° trap · Rev A · the single source of truth for CAD 
 Every part anchored to one coordinate frame with position, orientation, and size — so the model is built, not interpreted. Geometry, orientation, and fits are locked; force-driven values and production process are flagged. 
 1 · Datum coordinate system 
 Origin O = shaft-axis center = case mid-width, height 280 mm (COM), at the pivot's fore-aft inset. 
 +X = along the shaft, side-to-side (the 14″ width). The shaft axis is the X axis. 
 +Y = fore-aft; +Y points toward the case back / the user — the deploy direction. −Y = toward the front / packing. 
 +Z = vertical up. −Z = down toward the wheel base. 
 Deploy angle θ = measured in the Y–Z plane from +Z toward +Y . θ = 0° stowed (handle along +Z, in the recess); θ = 45° deployed. 
 Rule: a feature fixed on the hub at hub-angle φ sits at world angle (θ + φ). The stop lug is at φ = 135°, so at θ = 45° it points −Z (down). All rotations are about +X.
 2 · The pawl-on-hub correction 
Detailing the actuation exposed it: the trigger rod rides in the spine , i.e. on the moving hub. A pawl on the fixed bracket would make the push-only rod jump the moving/fixed boundary — it can't. The pawl is therefore on the hub , lifted directly by the trigger, held clear through the stow swing because it travels with the handle. The fold-back load is already in the hub, so handing it to a fixed bracket abutment is the natural path. Both stops are now "hub feature → fixed bracket feature": stop lug → fixed wall (open), hub pawl → fixed abutment (fold-back). This supersedes the pawl-on-bracket arrangement in the earlier pawl/bracket sheets.

 6 · Build order (deterministic) 
 Datum : planes X/Y/Z at O; the θ rotation axis = +X. 
 Shaft along X. LOCKED 
 Bracket : walls at ±25, floor on −Z, shaft bore, brace stub up +Z. Parent datum for the rest. 
 Fixed wall at the −Y pocket face; fixed abutment at the +Y pocket face — both absolute, at θ=45° geometry. Don't mirror. 
 Hub at O; spine boss radial at θ; stop lug at φ=135°. 
 Pawl + pin on the hub, fold-back side; friction disc on the +X hub face. 
 Drawbar collars at ±22, arm on the lower arc. 
 Assemble ; set θ=45°; sweep 0→45° and run the interference checks (§7). 
 7 · Sizing & interference 
 Loads (from the load spec): design 5,500 N at the r=20 contact (110 N·m snatch), ultimate 15,000 N (300 N·m yank). Pin Ø6 double-shear ~97 / 265 MPa; nose & seat bearing comfortable; release fights only the ~40 N return spring. All VALIDATE at FEA + bench. 
 Two interference checks the sweep must clear: 
1. −Z crowding: the stop lug + pawl sit at −Z at deploy, where the drawbar also stows. Clearance comes from radius and the drawbar lying flat to the back panel — confirm. 
2. Central axial band: the pawl + lug (central, X ±5) share the band with the hub (X ±9) and the friction disc (+X face). They clear by radius (pawl/lug outboard) and by putting the friction on the +X face away from the lug — confirm no clash with the inboard drawbar-collar edges.
 State of the joint: fully defined and oriented — origin, axes, every part's position/orientation/size, the parameter set, and the build order. Hand this to Zoo and it builds deterministically. The only things still open are the two interference sweeps (CAD), and the three force-driven numbers (fold-back impact, steel grade, spring rate) for the bench.
