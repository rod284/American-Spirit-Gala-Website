The Clutch, Split Three Ways

One part doing three jobs (and failing at it) → three parts, one job each.

Old — one clutch, three jobs

✗ Holding the load by friction needs thousands of N of clamp — no hand can squeeze that to release.

✗ Friction creeps under load — the handle slowly sags while you tow.

The Hirth-tooth interim fixed strength but was notchy, had to be pulled apart axially to re-angle (dragging in a cam + Bowden cable + return spring), and sat right on the EP2202128B1 prior art.

Now — three parts, one job each

Different jobs sit at different angles & arcs on the one hub — they never fight.

Job 1 · hold

Friction pack

Light multi-plate, ~6 interfaces, r≈16mm, ~300N clamp → a few N·m. Holds the angle only — handle weight + bumps. Squeeze-releasable; the concentrator backs it off.

Job 2 · load

45° hard stop

Hub lug seats into a hardened bracket face at full deploy. Takes the whole ~110 N·m tow/lift moment. Carries the load, not the angle.

Job 3 · stow

0° hook latch

Positive shoulder catches a fixed pin — bin-safe. Closing ramp auto-engages, ~15N seat spring, lifted by the trigger.

The rule: friction holds the angle · the stop takes the load · the hook handles stow. No clutch carries load anymore.
Why this is the doc correction. Your investor and patent materials still call it a "friction clutch" and reference the Hirth design. But nothing here is a clutch carrying load — and that wording would walk a patent claim straight into the EP2202128B1 Hirth prior art. The mechanism is a light friction angle-hold detent + a positive 45° deploy stop + a 0° stow hook. That's the language to put in front of patent counsel before the prior-art search.
