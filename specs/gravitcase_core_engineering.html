Hub zone (end view)
How the latch system shares the hub. Everything keys off one rotating hub on the fixed shaft. The spine boss swings the upper arc, 0°→45°; the 45° stop lug at the outer rim takes the tow/lift load into a hardened bracket face; the 0° hook on the hub grabs a fixed bracket pin for bin-safe stow; an annular friction pack holds the angle lightly; and the concentrator, fed by the trigger rod up the spine, backs the friction off and lifts the hook in one motion. The drawbar rides the same shaft on the lower arc — its yoke collars sit outboard near the walls (a wide base to resist cornering load) and straddle the hub. They never operate at once: handle up = lead case; drawbar out = towed case. The whole thing is one steel core that also extends up as the brace carrying the slide guides.
