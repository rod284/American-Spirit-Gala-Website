## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Neighbors Helping Neighbors (NHN) is a Scottsdale-based community organization that scales impact through active partnerships with 12+ vital organizations addressing homelessness, veteran support, food security, healthcare access, and community safety. The 2026 NHN Charity Gala will celebrate this collaborative mission while raising $150,000 to expand NHN's support for partner organizations.

This event leverages your 60+ active members—many of whom represent major employers (tech, healthcare, finance, real estate, insurance)—to create a high-impact evening of giving, networking, and community celebration.

---

## NHN'S MISSION & IMPACT STORY

**"We believe in the power of giving back to our community."**

Neighbors Helping Neighbors works to bring about positive change by:
- Making **substantial financial contributions** to partner organizations
- Providing **volunteer hours and hands-on support**
- Scaling impact through **collaborative partnerships** with organizations doing critical work
- Supporting communities where help is most needed

### NHN's 12 Vital Partner Programs:

1. **Rebuilding Together** — 8+ ADA ramps annually for accessibility
2. **UMOM - New Day Centers** — Ending homelessness through jobs + housing
3. **Shop with a Cop** — 9+ years providing holiday gifts for children in need
4. **National Memorial Cemetery of Arizona** — Honoring and maintaining veteran memorial sites
5. **Southwest Kids Cancer Foundation** — Supporting children and families affected by cancer
6. **Help (Homeless Engagement Lift Partnership)** — Breaking cycle of homelessness
7. **The Mana House** — Temporary housing and peer support for homeless veterans
8. **Camp Kesem** — Free summer camp for 150 children impacted by parent's cancer
9. **Scottsdale Beat the Heat Program** — Summer hydration & wellness checks
10. **Operation Fix It** — Code enforcement & safety repairs for low-income homeowners
11. **St. Mary's Food Bank** — 200,000+ meals provided to community
12. **Additional Local Partners** — Youth development, emergency services, health access
